Mustafa Kemal Atatürk

The man who saved and modernized a nation.

Here's a time line of Mustafa Kemal Atatürk's life:

  • 1881 - Mustafa Kemal Atatürk born in Salonica.
  • 1893 - Mustafa enters military preparatory school in Salonica.
  • 1899 - Mustafa Kemal enters infantry class of War College in Istanbul
  • 1902 - Commissioned Second Lieutenant, and enters Staff College.
  • 1905 - Passes out as Staff Captain and is posted for Fifth Army in Syria; revives a secret opposition group in Damascus.
  • 1913 - Mustafa Kemal appointed military attaché in Sofia.
  • 1915 - Mustafa Kemal leaves Sofia to take up appointment as commander of 19th division for service in Battle of Gallipoli.
  • 1919 - Appointed the Inspector of the Ninth Army Troops.
  • 1919 - Kemal lands in Samsun.
  • 1919 - Mustafa Kemal resigns from the post of Inspector of Third Army and from the army.
  • 1919 - Kemal elected Chairman of Erzurum Congress.
  • 1920 - Kemal opens the Grand National Assembly (BMM) in Ankara
  • 1920 - Kemal is condemned to death by the government in Constantinople.
  • 1921 - Appointed Commander - in - Chief by the Grand National Assembly.
  • 1921 - The battle of Sakarya begins with Turkish troops led by Mustafa Kemal.
  • 1921 - Kemal receives the rank of Marshal and the title Gazi (veteran, victorious warrior).
  • 1922 - Gazi Mustafa Kemal begins to lead Great Offensive from the hill of Kocatepe.
  • 1922 - Mustafa Kemal at the Battle of Dumlupınar.
  • 1922 - Mustafa Kemal Atatürk born in Salonica.
  • 1922 - Enters Izmir.
  • 1922 - The Grand National Assembly abolish the Sultanate.
  • 1923 - Proclamation of the Republic of Turkey.
  • 1923 - Elected first president.
  • 1924 - The Caliphate is abolished, the Ottoman dynasty is exiled, religious schools are closed down, and organized Islam becomes regulated by the state.
  • 1924 - Instating the new Turkish Civil Code and other legislation to suit secular order
  • 1924 - Integration of education.
  • 1925 - The Revolution of Headgear and Outfit.
  • 1925 - Adoption of the International calendar, time and measurements.
  • 1926 - Women were given equal rights with men.
  • 1928 - Adoption of the new Turkish alphabet.
  • 1933 - Organization of the university education.
  • 1934 - The surname law.
  • 1934 - First Five-Year Plan for industrial development.
  • 1934 - Balkan Pact concluded between Turkey, Greece, Rumania and Yugoslavia.
  • 1934 - Mustafa Kemal adopts the surname "ATATÜRK" which is given to him by the Turkish Parliament.
  • 1934 - Women made eligible to vote in Parliamentary elections and to become members of Parliament.
  • 1938 - The death of Mustafa Kemal “Ataturk,” the leader of the Turkish War of Independence and founder of modern-day Turkey.

"If Ataturk had lived today the world would have been a more interesting place. When, in 1915, during our ill-fated Dardanelles campaign fate brought us across in Gallipoli we immediately knew that we were up against a military genius and a formidable strategist. Soon he emerged as a noble warrior. Later he proved himself as a genuine peace-maker and a consummate statesman as well."
--Winston Churchill, British P.M.


"Ataturk, with his historic achievements, was one of the greatest leaders of this century (20th). He was a towering figure of human ideals. His extraordinary leadership and his military genius accomplished so much for his country; first in battlefields, and then in building his nation, materially and in spirit. He created a modern and sovereign state from the ashes of a defeated empire. He was a great peace-maker, an ultimate reformer and a torch-bearer of freedom. He won not only the heart of his nation but the hearts of the oppressed nations as well. We all miss him."
--John F. Kennedy, President of the United States


"Because of the 25th year of Atatürk's death, I want to express the feelings of loyal friendship felt for the Turkish nation by the French nation. Today, Turkish history even more than ever is inseparable from Western and European history. Atatürk's efforts in this direction were not left without results. The friendship between our countries that has surpassed hundreds of years, has formed the foundation for this development."
--Charles de GAULLE, President of France
